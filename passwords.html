Password Policy and Documentation

1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

At NovaStream, we prioritize the security of our digital assets and recognize that passwords play a crucial role in safeguarding sensitive information. This policy aims to establish strong password practices to mitigate potential risks and protect our systems from unauthorized access.

1.2 Scope

This policy covers all systems and data within the NovaStream network, including servers, databases, applications, and user data. Also, this policy may cover data located on third party servers, databases and applications utilized by NovaStream.

2. Requirements

2.1 Complexity

  • Passwords must be atleast 12 characters long.
  • Passwords must include a combination of the following:
    • Uppercase letters (A-Z)
    • Lowercase letters (a-z)
    • Numbers (0-9)
    • Special characters (e.g., !, @, #, $, %, ^, &).

    You can exclude ambigious characters (e.g., L, l, i, I, 0, o) that may have trouble interpreting on different screens or fonts.

2.2 Reuse

Users are prohibited from using the same or a similiar password across different services or platforms. This includes your email password.

The use of unique passwords for NovaStream systems and any connected third-party services is mandatory.

3. Password Rotations

3.1 Frequency

As per 2023 NIST 800-63 guidelines, we do not recommend any enforcement on password rotations or dates of mandatory password changes.

NIST recommends that businesses enforce password expiration and password resets only when a known compromise has occurred, or every 365 days.

3.2 Reuse

Users cannot reuse any of their last five (5) passwords. If the option is available in the software or platform, it must be enabled.

4. Account Lockouts

When possible, enable account locking after multiple failed attempts.

4.1 Timespan

If the application or service allows, five (5) failures in a short timespan should lock the account for a minimum of 30 minutes. This should be reviewed based on the priority of the account.

4.2 Notification

If possible, enable notification of account lockout to the account owner.

5. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA/2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) codes are temporary, time-sensitive passcodes generated during the login process. Typically delivered through methods like SMS, authenticator apps, or push notifications, these codes provide an additional layer of security by requiring users to enter a second factor, along with their password, for authentication.

We encourage the use of multi-factor authentication of atleast two (2) factors for additional layers of security.

5.2 Priority

5.3 MFA Fatigue

Be wary of MFA attempts when mutliple requests come in, or at odd times. Contact the security team if you are receiving MFA requests not requested by you. Do not allow if you are unsure it was requested by you.

5.4 Backup Codes

2FA recovery/backup codes are static, pre-generated backup codes intended for use when a user is unable to access their primary 2FA method. Unlike dynamic 2FA codes, recovery codes remain constant and are securely stored by the user. When needed, it serves as a reliable fallback mechanism in case of emergencies or when the primary authentication method is unavailable.

5.4.1 Storage

  • Recovery codes used to restore access to an account MUST NOT be stored in a password manager or in a document, spreadsheet, etc on a computer.
  • Recovery codes should be written or printed off, labelled, dated and stored in a secure location.

5.4.3 Rotations

Recovery codes should be changed based on the "Password Rotation" section in this document.

6. Storage & Providing Access

6.1 Storage

  • Passwords must be stored securely using industry-standard encryption methods.
  • We encourage the use of a password manager such as BitWarden as well as enabling 2FA on that account.
  • Avoid storing passwords in plaintext such as in documents, emails, or spreadsheets.
  • When developing software that requires passwords, passwords must be stored as a hash in the database or storage provider.

6.2 Providing Access

7. Education & Training

6.1 Education

Staff and clients should be educated about the importance of strong passwords and avoiding common passwords.

6.2 Training

In addition to general password training, we may conduct regular security awareness training to educate employees about the importance of maintaining strong password practices.

Staff who fail security audits or do not follow this policy may be required to retake training sessions.

8. Account Authorization

  • We regularly review, audit and modify user accounts and access permissions when deemed necessary.
  • We will disable and/or remove accounts promptly when an employee leaves NovaStream.
  • For clients and their platforms, we only disable and/or remove accounts when requested by an authorized user of said company. The request must be provided in writing and a secondary confirmation must be received before initiating the action.

9. Password Recovery

  • Platforms and software that require an account utilized or created by NovaStream must provide a secure and reliable password recovery process, ensuring it doesn't compromise security.
  • Password recovery requested by clients must be sent in writing by the user of the account from the email address associated with the account or by an authorized member of the company. A secondary confirmation should be requested to reduce impersonation or phishing attacks.

10. Remote Access & Screen Sharing

  • For remote access to our devices and platforms, use of a Virtual Private Network (VPN) is required and ensure strong authentication methods.
  • You must not allow remote connections to your devices without authorization.
  • Before screen sharing, please make sure your password manager is secure and any confidential information/applications is hidden or closed.

11. Third-party Services

  • You should ensure that third-party services and applications integrated with company systems meet security standards.
  • If the service is not mission-critial or does not meet security standards, we recommend using an email masking service and using fake/dummy data unrelated to the company or the individual.
  • Passwords should be unique and not reused by any of NovaStream acccount passwords to the third-party services.

12. Monitoring/Logging & Reporting

12.1 Logging

  • Implement monitoring and logging of login attempts and unusual activities.
  • Log files must be available for a minimum of six (6) months.

12.2 Reporting

Employees play a vital role in identifying and reporting potential security threats or incidents promptly. If you observe any suspicious activity, encounter a potential security vulnerability, or have reason to believe that your account has been compromised, we encourage you to do the following:

  1. Report any security concerns or incidents as soon as they are identified. Time is of the essence in responding to potential threats, and prompt reporting enables the security team to take swift action.
  2. Utilize the designated reporting channels established by the company. This means contacting the assigned security contact person, Matthew Lewis through email using the address matt@novastream.ca. No other methods of communication are allowed.
  3. Provide detailed information about the observed security concern. Include relevant context, such as the nature of the incident, the systems or accounts involved, and any potential impact if known.
  4. Reports can be submitted anonymously if preferred, and confidentiality will be maintained to the extent allowed by law.
  5. Our incident response team will promptly assess and address reported security concerns. In the case of confirmed incidents, appropriate measures will be taken to contain, mitigate, and investigate the situation.
  6. If necessary, the team will produce a postmortem report which documents the details and outcome to the investigation. This can be reviewed and shared to the individual whom reported the incident. This may also be shared with employees, company owners or shareholders for transparency.

13. Documentation

13.1 Incidents

Any individual handling incidents related to passwords or password reset requests should be documented and logged. At minimum, it should have the ticket number if possible, details/reasoning, date and time, the affected account, and who handled the incident.

If it was a requested reset, it should also include information about the requester and the confirmation.
